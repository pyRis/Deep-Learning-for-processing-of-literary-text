The wrestler, however, is resolutely dissatisfied with the pronouncements of his master, and decides to execute the wrestler if he doesn't come to-morrow. He wants to marry and become the heir to the throne of his father, but fears for his position. When he breaks the endorsement, he promises to rejoice in his father's honor, which he initially refuses. However, when he learns that his father has no heir to the throne, he promises to rejoice in his father's honor again.
