A messenger appears and asks the musicians to tell him about the singer's singing. First Musician answers by saying that he is a merchant and demanding that the singer should pay him a visit. The second Musician tells the singer to bring the minstrel and the serving-creature, while the first Musician demands that he should give them the serving-creature and the goldsmith's diadem. Balthasar sends a letter to the queen to tell her that she is in good health and nothing can be said about it. However, he urges the queen to get ink and paper and hire post-horses to tell the queen how to get home.
