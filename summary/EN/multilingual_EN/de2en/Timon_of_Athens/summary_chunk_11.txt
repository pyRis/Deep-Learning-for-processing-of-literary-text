The servant is about to be delivered to a house of Lord Timon's servants. When he is asked to get the wine, he tells the servants to empty the wine in order to prepare a dish for himself and for his master, who is a well-beingeded gentleman of Athens. The money is stolen from Fly , but he claims to be better than his master. When Fly learns about the deed , he demands the money back from Flemish. When the deed is expelled , he threatens to be executed . When the deed is found , he orders LUCILIUS and three other strangers to execution , and when the deed is pronounced dead , he orders them to be driven from the house of Timon .
