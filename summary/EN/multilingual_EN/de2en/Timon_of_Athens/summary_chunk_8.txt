The Sicilian king Leontes is angry at the Sicilian lord, because his sons, Antipholus and Lords, have been arrested and accused of adultery by the Sicilian court. He demands from Antipholus and Antipholus, who is also arrested and accused of adultery, that he should be released immediately. He demands from Antipholus and Antipholus to pay the outstanding debts. The fool with the name Apemantus is being pursued by the raging bastard.
