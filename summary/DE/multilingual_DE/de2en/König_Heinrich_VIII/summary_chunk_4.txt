The king's court orders the nobleman Douglas Macbeth to give in to his heir to the throne in order to unlock up the king's sword in search of his heir to the throne. The Duke of Edinburgh meets the Duke of Scotland and demands from him that he tells his servants three things from the Duke of Ephesus: first, he tells them about his divine plan, second, about his divine will and finally, third, about his ambition to become a successor of the Duke of Scotland.
